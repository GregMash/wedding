Our Story

Her Side

I had never experienced that feeling I heard so often: “When you know, you just know.” I came to the conclusion that was hopeless romantic talk. Despite this, I didn’t give up the idea of meeting my person. I downloaded Bumble and gave myself one week to check it out. Good looks aside, Greg immediately caught my attention with his U of A alumni status. We both swiped right and voila- a match meant to bee. (Thank you, Bumble) I reached out with a “Bear Down” and our communication seamlessly continued. It was so easy to talk to Greg and soon enough, he was planning our first date.

Greg was the ultimate gentleman and picked me up. When I saw him standing outside my apartment, I was pleasantly surprised. I saw a 6’1” handsome man walking towards me. (He didn’t mention his height. In my head- YES!!) Greg’s easy going attitude eased my nerves. Our first date was at an Italian restaurant where we ended up closing the place down. The date flew by and I remember when we said goodbye, he gave me the best hug. I walked up to my apartment and couldn’t stop smiling! I had really hoped he felt the same way. I wanted to text him and thank him for an amazing evening, but I didn’t want to seem too hasty. Instead, I called my mom to share and while we were talking, I receive a text from Greg that I have saved to this day. He said to hell with the rules and said exactly what I wanted to say to him!

It has been right from the very beginning. I was finally excited to say I believe in “When you know, you just know.” So thankful for our story and can’t wait to spend the rest of my life with my very best friend.

His Side

From the first time Laura appeared on my phone, all felt right. It began with lighthearted communication leading up to our first date. Then came the excitement and nervousness of that unforgettable night. Our connection was instantaneous and I experienced a feeling that was completely new to me. Soon after that night, we began dating and I would come to understand what that feeling was: falling in love and finding my person.

Every day, I am still amazed by the exponential growth of that feeling.

We spent the ensuing years deepening our love and becoming a part of each other's families. It's funny how so many things in life are complicated, but our relationship has been the greatest, easiest, and most sensible thing I have experienced.

Laura and I lived in Denver for two years before briefly returning to Arizona. Recently, we landed ourselves back in Colorado, this time in Colorado Springs. I could not be more happy, proud, and excited to live out the rest of our adventures together.

Laura and I spend our time traveling, hiking, skiing/snowboarding, listening to live music, and spending time with friends & family every chance we get. I have never had more fun than when I am with her and absolutely can't wait to spend my whole life with Laura.

Laura and Greg will be married at Saguaro Buttes, a beautiful and secluded location overlooking the Saguaro National Park.

The Engagement

Greg proposed at the Bison Golf Club near the Mash family cabin. Seeing Greg on one knee with my stunning ring felt surreal. I said absolutely and he continued to surprise me with both of our families awaiting at a nearby park. We all joyously celebrated with an amazing brunch. I will never forget that beautiful day full of love and family!

Getting To Know The Bride & Groom

For those of our family and friends who do not know both the bride and groom, we wanted to provide a quick glimpse into their lives.

The Bride

Laura grew up in Flagstaff, AZ. Her loving parents, Mike and Jennifer, still live in northern Arizona and have been married for over 35 years. She had a joyful childhood patrolling the neighborhood with her younger brother, Brett, and best friend, Kimberly. Laura excelled in piano and academics where she earned a scholarship to the University of Arizona. She graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Nursing with Psychology and Spanish minors. In college, she discovered a love for global health where she became a volunteer and later coordinator for a HIV/AIDS education program in Tanzania, East Africa. Her first trip to Tanzania in 2010 changed her life forever. Her homestay family in a Maasai village called Shiboro became one of her own and they continue to maintain a strong bond with holiday phone calls sharing a lot of laughter, broken Swahili and English, and singing. Laura was honored when they named twins after she and her father. She will always love her Tanzanian family and can't wait for Greg to meet them. Laura has extensively traveled abroad and loves to learn about new cultures. She has been working as a nurse for 8 years and currently works in the ER.
